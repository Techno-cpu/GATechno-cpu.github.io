ROUND 1: Warm-Up (Multiple Choice Questions)

  1. Who is known as the “Father of Geography”?
    a) Claudius Ptolemy b) Alexander von Humboldt c) Eratosthenes d) Abraham Ortelius
  2. What is a degree of longitude at the equator in kilometers?
    a) 111 b) 51 c) 360 d) 180
  3. The Equator divides Earth into:
    a) Eastern and Western b) North and South Hemispheres c) Tropics d) Deserts
  4. Which line runs through Greenwich, England?
    a) Tropic of Cancer b) 90° latitude c) Prime Meridian d) Equator
  5. How many time zones does the world have?
    a) 12 b) 24 c) 60 d) 360
  6. The intersection of Equator and Prime Meridian is called:
    a) Null Island b) Crossroads c) Grid Point d) Zero Town
  7. What is the latitude of the North Pole?
    a) 0° b) 23.5° c) 66.5° d) 90°
  8. Which circles are NOT great circles?
    a) Equator b) All longitudes c) All latitudes except Equator d) Prime Meridian
  9. Who created the first modern atlas?
    a) Eratosthenes b) Abraham Ortelius c) Mercator d) Humboldt
  10. The first global navigation map was drawn by:
    a) Mercator b) Columbus c) Idrisi d) Da Vinci
  11. Which measurement gives the distance north or south of the Equator?
    a) Longitude b) Latitude c) Altitude d) Gradient
  12. What is the origin (0°, 0°) point of Earth’s grid called humorously?
    a) Null Island b) Origination Point c) Meridian Town d) Zero Bay
  13. How many main directions are on a compass?
    a) 2 b) 3 c) 4 d) 8
  14. Which geographer first estimated Earth's circumference?
    a) Magellan b) Eratosthenes c) Tharp d) Humboldt
  15. What imaginary line passes through both poles?
    a) Equator b) Latitude c) Longitude/Prime Meridian d) Tropic of Capricorn
